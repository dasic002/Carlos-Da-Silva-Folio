McDs drive thru handle

Brief

The previous payment device (PDQ) at McDonalds branches , the Ingenico IPP350, was coming to the end of its lifecycle and a new reach extender pole was required to hold the new PDQ, the Ingenico LANE3000 so it is easy to pass the device to the driver and complete the payment.

Electronic Kensington Slot locks

Brief

Ergonomic Solutions are a well established manufacturer of hardware to support the Point-Of-Sale, offering mounts, cradles, locks and more to customise the checkout setup. With the market leading towards more intelligent and connected hardware, ES wanted to convert their existing mechanical locks to electronic ones, whilst maintaining the form factor as close as to the original to enable backward compatibility. The locks and framework needed to support an easier “Key” management system, whereby HQ could authorise certain levels of staff to operate assets on the field, as well as logging of lock operations on a cloud system, all the meanwhile making the hardware compact, reliable and easy to operate.

Unique Secure

Brief

During the employment at Unique Secure Ltd, the flagship product has been the MePOS, Modular electronic Point of Sale. The modularity enabled our clients to deploy their choice of Tablet computer, Pin entry device, Receipt printer, as well as configure the orientation of the POS system (i.e.: Traditional, Self-serve, and mobile).
A great portion of projects were creating new modules, primarily Tablet skins and and PED harnesses, along with some other accessories.

Self4000 Mount

Brief

McDs were looking to install a payment device that is permanently fixed outside the pay booth of drive thru branches, in an attempt to reduce queueing times. The member staff would have to reach out with the PDQ fewer times to the customer and be able to focus on taking the next order. This has to consider typical engineer install conditions/access, extreme weather it may face, the risk of it being hit by cars and the ergonomics in driver operation.
